Community Engagement

Unearthed stories belong to everyone.

Mechanical Maniacs partner with museums, tribal storytellers, and classrooms so that the artifacts we highlight reflect the people who protect them.

Museum pop-ups

We run weekend discovery tables at the Museum of Seminole County History where visitors scan LEGO-built replicas and hear audio clips recorded by elders.

Library maker labs

In partnership with the Orange County Library System, we lend scanning rigs so families can digitize heirlooms and add them to a shared Unearthed gallery.

Dig kits for classrooms

Elementary students borrow mini excavation boxes that teach stratigraphy, artifact tagging, and scientific sketches.

Storytelling nights

We host bilingual interview nights with the Central Florida Anthropological Society so neighbors can archive stories before hurricane season.

Engagement goals

  • Document 50 new oral histories tied to community artifacts
  • Train 200 students to handle delicate objects respectfully
  • Share Unearthed field kits with every Title I school in Seminole County
  • Publish an online archive so rural partners can contribute remotely
